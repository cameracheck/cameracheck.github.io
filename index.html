Process

How the Camera Test Works

Three steps to complete camera diagnostics — no account, no install, no data shared.

01

Grant Browser Permission

When you click "Start Test," your browser asks for camera (and optionally microphone) access. Click Allow — this permission stays local on your device and is revocable anytime from browser settings.

02

Live Stream Begins

Using the WebRTC API, your browser opens a direct stream from your camera to the on-page video element. No data leaves your computer — the stream is processed entirely client-side in JavaScript.

03

Read Diagnostics

Resolution, FPS, device name, aspect ratio, and facing mode are all extracted from the MediaStream API and displayed in real time on your screen. Switch cameras, test your mic, or capture a screenshot.

Help Center

Camera Not Working? Fix It Here

The most common camera problems and their solutions — covering every major browser and operating system.

🚫

Permission Denied

Your browser blocked camera access. Click the camera icon in the address bar → select "Allow" → refresh the page and try again.

🔒

Camera In Use

Another app (Zoom, Teams, OBS) may be using the camera. Close all video apps, then restart your browser before running the test.

⚙️

Driver Issues

Outdated or corrupt drivers on Windows can prevent detection. Open Device Manager → Cameras → Update driver → reboot.

🌐

HTTP vs HTTPS

WebRTC camera access requires a secure HTTPS connection. If you're on HTTP, the browser will always block camera permissions.

📱

iOS Restrictions

On iPhone/iPad, camera access in Safari works — but third-party browsers may be limited. Always use Safari on iOS for best results.

🛡️

Antivirus Blocking

Some security software blocks camera access for all browsers. Temporarily disable your antivirus webcam shield and test again.
